Why Vermicompost

It's not fertilizer. It's living soil.

Chemical fertilizers feed the plant for a season. Vermicompost feeds the soil for years — building structure, microbial life, and water retention that lasts well beyond a single harvest.

Microbial life, restored

Each gram carries millions of beneficial microbes that rebuild soil biology depleted by years of chemical use.

Holds water like a sponge

Vermicompost can retain several times its weight in moisture, meaning fewer watering cycles and stronger drought resistance.

Slow, steady nutrition

Nutrients release gradually as microbes break them down — no burn risk, no nutrient runoff into groundwater.

How It's Made

Waste in, living soil out.

Every batch follows the same unhurried process. We don't rush worms.

1

Collect & sort

Vegetable scraps, dry leaves, and cow dung are sourced locally and sorted before feeding.

2

Feed the beds

Red wigglers break the material down over several weeks, turning it into fine castings.

3

Cure & sieve

Finished compost rests for stability, then is hand-sieved to remove bedding and stray worms.

4

Pack & deliver

Bagged fresh to order — we don't keep large stock sitting around, so what you get is recently cured.

"You can tell good compost by the smell — it should smell like a forest floor after rain, never sour or ammonia-like. That's the standard we hold every batch to."

— Arun Kumar Sharma, Founder, Ojasvi Organic
